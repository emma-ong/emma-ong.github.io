Discuss what it is and why it is relevant

Fixed mindset

  • Believing that intelligence is not developable
  • Deems failure as a reflection of one’s intelligence
  • Ignores feedback that is constructive
  • Feels intimidated when others succeed
  • Blames own failure on external factors such as the outcome of the actions by others

Growth Mindset

  • Believing that intelligence is changeable
  • Learns and grows from failure
  • Views failure as a form of motivation to put in more effort in learning
  • Leverages constructive feedback to improve self and others
  • Does not blame others for their own failures and looked for means to improve instead
Relevance of a Growth Mindset
  • There is more emphasis on one's progress over being perfect. When pushed out of one's comfort zone there is an understanding for those with a growth mindset, that improvements comes from effort and hard work, so the main focus is the incremental changes in progress and the journey itself.
  • Developing a growth mindset also puts one in a resilient position when it comes to dealing with obstacles and their reaction towards them. The practice of compassion and patience with oneself, irrespective of setbacks, creates a space where failures are part of the journey and fundamental in the achievement of personal and professional growth.

In this exploration, did anything surprise you? Change for you?

I learned about the fluidity of both fixed and growth mindsets, and how they are points on a spectrum dependent on the situation. For example, a student may find they have more of a fixed mindset in their academic studies and but more of a growth mindset in physical education classes. The ideal would be to adopt a growth mindset in as many situations as possible, though it would often be a persistent work in progress and a need to have awareness of how their mindsets can influence their measure of success.

How will you integrate growth mindset into your learning journey?

  • To focus and be open to different solutions rather than dwelling on the same problem
  • Encouraging and reminding others and myself, at points of struggle and being overwhelmed, that the feeling is momentary and will pass
